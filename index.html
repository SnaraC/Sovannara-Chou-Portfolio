About Me

I am a highly motivated and detail-oriented student of Computer Science and Software Engineering at the University of Washington Bothell, with a unique perspective and diverse background.

I was born and raised in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, and in 2017, I moved to the United States. My educational path commenced at Edmond College, where I initially pursued a degree in computer science. However, due to my family circumstances, I transitioned to a mechatronics technical degree. After successfully obtaining my mechatronics degree at Everett Community College and finishing a mechatronics internship at Boeing, I came to realize that my passion for computer science remained strong within me. I decided to come back to Edmond College, where I resumed my studies to earn a transfer degree to the University of Washington Bothell.

My personal experiences have inspired in me the values of hard work, perseverance, and adaptability, which I apply to my academic and professional pursuits.

I am currently seeking for an internship and entry software engineering job. I am committed to developing innovative solutions and producing high-quality work, and I believe that my diverse background and unique perspective make me a valuable asset to any team.
